How much should I pay for a web site?

What you pay depends on what you expect to get out of your site, plus the amount and quality of service you're going to receive from the designer/developer. Cheap is great, as long you're okay with template sites, minimal design and not much service

The important thing is to know exactly what you're getting from a technical aspect, design aspect, level of quality, services included, SEO considerations, content development, and ongoing support. Compare estimates fairly and ask lots of questions before accepting the lowest bid. We like to relate the value of a project to the return you expect. Some projects will be high, some low. Focus on the results you expect and work with a team you trust to get the job done.

What's the difference between responsive web site and a mobile site?

A responsive web site is one that is designed to scale to fit on multiple screen sizes e.g., computer monitors, widescreens, tablets, even mobile phones. Responsive design is a great way to ensure your content is seen correctly on many devices.

A true "mobile site" is different in that there is a unique set of code and sometimes content used to create a page. This means you can further customize the experience for mobile users. Generally a responsive site is good for most websites, and in general it is the method that Google prefers for search indexing. However for large, complex sites, and those that may be media heavy, a mobile version may be better.
